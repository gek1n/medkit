1. Introduction

This Privacy Policy explains what data the MedKit app ("we", "the app") processes and how. MedKit is built around one principle: your health data belongs to you alone. The app requires no registration, has no user accounts, and our server is deliberately designed to see as little as possible — ideally nothing readable at all.

This is not medical advice. MedKit is a reminder and organization tool, not a medical device and not a substitute for a doctor, pharmacist, or emergency care. See the Terms of Service for details.
🕶️

2. Core principle: a blind server

No accounts — nothing to leak

MedKit has no sign-up, no email/password, no social login, and no central user database. This is a deliberate architectural choice: if there are no accounts, there's no risk of a user-database breach.

Where the app does need a server (syncing between your own devices, voice commands), the server only ever sees:

  • Encrypted data blobs, whose decryption key exists only on your devices;
  • Random technical identifiers (e.g. a pairing code or channel ID) that are meaningless on their own;
  • A device push token — needed solely to deliver notifications via Firebase Cloud Messaging.

The server never sees, in readable form: your name, medication names, dosages, schedules, photos, health status, symptoms, or any other content.

📱

3. Data stored only on your device

Everything you enter in MedKit — medications, schedules, family members, wellbeing check-ins, doctor appointments, package photos — is stored locally only, in an encrypted database on your device (SQLCipher, AES-256). Photos are additionally encrypted with a separate key (AES-256-GCM).

Encryption keys are stored in your operating system's secure enclave (Keychain on iOS, Keystore on Android) and never leave the device in plaintext.

App access can optionally be protected by biometrics (Face ID / fingerprint) or your device passcode.

Consequence: if you uninstall the app without first creating a backup, your data is lost permanently. We keep no server-side copy we could restore on your behalf.

5. AI features and third-party providers

Voice commands — Anthropic (Claude)

If you use voice input, the text of your command (e.g. "took paracetamol") is sent to our server, which forwards it to Anthropic's Claude model to recognize the intended action. Before first use, the app explicitly asks for your consent and names Anthropic as the provider.

Free-text descriptions of wellbeing or symptoms are never sent to the cloud — those entries stay on your device only.

Prescription and package scanning — Anthropic (Claude)

A photo of a doctor's prescription or a medication package (one or several at once) is sent to our server, which forwards it to Anthropic's Claude model to recognize medication names, dosages, and brief reference information (timing relative to food, possible side effects). Before first use, the app explicitly asks for a separate consent and names Anthropic as the provider. The photo is used only for recognition and is not stored anywhere afterward.

The food-timing and side-effect reference information is not sourced from a vetted medical catalog — it comes from the model's general knowledge. The app always labels it as indicative only and recommends checking the package insert.

6. Backups (Google Drive / iCloud)

You may optionally create a backup in your own Google Drive (Android) or iCloud (iOS). The backup is written to a hidden, app-private storage area — not your regular visible file list.

Data in the backup is already encrypted the same way it is on your device. The encryption keys are additionally wrapped with a password you choose when creating the backup. Neither MedKit, Google, nor Apple can read the backup's contents without that password — and we never store it anywhere. If you forget it, the backup cannot be recovered.

🔗

7. Cross-device sharing (pairing)

To share a schedule with another device (e.g. showing a child a parent's schedule), the two devices exchange a one-time code. Data is encrypted on one device with a key derived from that code, and decrypted only on the second device after the same code is entered. The server merely holds the encrypted blob for a short time — it cannot be read without the code.

8. Dependent profiles and children

MedKit lets you add profiles for family members (e.g. children or elderly parents) managed by the device owner. These are not separate accounts — dependent profiles have no login of their own and no direct access to the app unless the profile owner sets that up themselves.

The adult managing the app is responsible for the accuracy and appropriateness of any data entered for a child's or other dependent's profile.

⚖️

9. Your rights

Since the bulk of your data lives only on your device, most rights (access, correction, deletion) are exercised directly in the app — you edit or delete records yourself, without needing to ask us.

  • Deleting all data: uninstalling the app from your device (and manually removing any backup from Drive/iCloud) removes all of your data completely.
  • Server-side data: transient technical data (pairing blobs, push tokens) can be deleted on request via the email below — though most of it expires automatically within minutes or hours anyway.
🛡️

10. Security

  • Local database encrypted (SQLCipher, AES-256)
  • Photos encrypted with a separate key (AES-256-GCM, a unique nonce per file)
  • Optional biometric protection for app access
  • All server communication over HTTPS/TLS only
  • Server-side rate limiting against abuse and code guessing
